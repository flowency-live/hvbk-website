What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies:

  • Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them

How We Use Cookies

The Hi Vis Bookkeeper website uses a minimal number of cookies. We believe in keeping things simple and only use cookies that are necessary or genuinely useful.

Third-Party Cookies

We minimise the use of third-party services that set cookies. Currently:

  • Google Fonts: We load fonts from Google Fonts. Google may collect usage data. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Supabase: Our contact form uses Supabase for secure data storage. Supabase may set technical cookies necessary for form submission.

We do not use analytics cookies, advertising cookies, or social media tracking cookies.

Your Cookie Choices

You have several options for managing cookies:

Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • See what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

Chrome Firefox Safari Edge

Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable essential cookies, some features of this website may not work correctly. For example:

  • The contact form may not submit properly
  • Your cookie preference may not be remembered

Similar Technologies

In addition to cookies, websites may use similar technologies:

  • Local Storage: Similar to cookies but can store more data. We do not currently use local storage for tracking.
  • Web Beacons: Small images that track page views. We do not use web beacons.

Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that signals to websites that you do not want to be tracked. As we do not use tracking or analytics cookies, this setting does not affect how our website operates.
